About Me

My name is Dillon Gyotoku. I am a junior in the Industrial Engineering program at the University of Washington in Seattle, Washington who is also pursing a specialization in Data Science. I was born and raised just outside Seattle in Newcastle, but also frequently travel down to Honolulu, Hawaii to visit my dad's side of the family.

I am currently working part-time as a coding instructor, teaching C# through Unity online. I have taught students in Java, JavaScript, Lua, HTML/CSS, C#, and Scratch. I chose this position to improve my coding skills and become exposed to a variety of languages which could help me in any engineering field. As a longtime employee and team leader, I work directly with the business owner and my coworkers to coordinate class schedules, assist students, and create curriculum for our summer camps.

In the past year, I also became a research assistant at the University of Washington's Mechatronics, Automation, and Control Systems (MACS) Lab. I'm currently working on analyzing laser powder bed fusion images for defects using machine learning models trained using Python, specifically the PyTorch libary. This project has given me a brand new experience working with and analyzing new types of data that I wouldn't see in a classroom setting, and presents exactly the kind of problems that first drew me to Industrial Engineering. Academically, I am committed to my education and excel in my studies with a GPA of 3.80. Some of my favorite courses so far have been Technical Writing, Manufacturing Systems, and Data Programming.

In addition to class and work, I attend clubs and generally seek out opportunities to expand my knowledge. I obtained my Six Sigma Green Belt certification in January 2022, and I've gotten the chance to test my Python skills in the MACS lab. I am experienced with using Excel, and also recently began branching out to learning R. My current goal is to accumulate experience with simulation programs such as Simio and FlexSim throughout the fall quarter and beyond.

In my free time, I enjoy reading, listening to music, exercising, and playing video games.

Analytical Writing Samples

Machine Learning Model Analysis

This piece was written as a solo project in the summer of 2022. As part of my Data Programming class, I was asked to work with an external dataset and go beyond the bounds of the class to learn something new in Python. I was able to combine this with my work in the MACS lab, and I chose to compare and contrast different cropping strategies and model frameworks to determine if changing these elements produced a more accurate and therefore more useful model. All data processing and analysis was written by me, and my mentor at the MACS lab assisted during the training process to make sure that the models were viable for comparison.

Business CSR Report

This piece was written with one partner during January of 2019. We were asked to investigate and analyze the corporate social responsibility of a local dentist's office, and then propose a plan for how the business could improve its corporate social responsibility. For this project, my partner and I interviewed the business owner, then surveyed both customers and employees and used the data to create the Eastview Community Outreach Plan. This plan included a 6-month timeline, budget, and analysis of the business and its surrounding environment, and recommended various activities for the business to improve its corporate social responsibility. I handled the data analysis using Excel and was responsible for interviewing the business owner, and worked with my partner on all of the report material. We presented this plan to the business owner and at DECA competitions, where we finished as Washington State Finalists.
